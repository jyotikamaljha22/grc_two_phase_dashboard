Global Two-Phase Immersion Cooling Market, 2025–2035

Two-phase immersion cooling is technically powerful but commercially selective. Its strongest opportunity lies in extreme-density AI/HPC and selected high-performance data-center use cases, while adoption will depend on dielectric fluid economics, serviceability, customer confidence, OEM alignment, and competition from direct-to-chip and single-phase immersion cooling.

3. Revenue Layer Split

Market value structurally shifts from initial hardware capital expenditure toward lifecycle fluid management and recurring services.

Tank and enclosure hardware share compresses from 44% in 2025 to 38% in 2035 due to scale manufacturing. Conversely, the necessity for fluid testing, top-ups, and specialized maintenance expands the recurring service layer from 7% to 11%.

The strongest opportunity lies not just in metal fabrication, but in controlling the complex fluid ecosystem. For GRC, this highlights a critical competitive vector: positioning single-phase as a lower-risk, lower-OPEX fluid environment compared to boiling dielectric systems.

4. Application Opportunity Split

Deployment is highly asymmetric. Mainstream enterprise and edge environments present minimal opportunity due to infrastructure complexity, whereas high-density nodes are the primary engine.

  • ▪ AI/HPC: Captures the vast majority of net-new demand.
  • ▪ Hyperscale: Selective adoption for bespoke, high-compute zones.

Two-phase systems are commercially viable almost exclusively where rack densities cross 100 kW. GRC can defend the vast 30–80 kW mid-density colocation/enterprise tier by emphasizing single-phase operational familiarity and sufficient thermal headroom.

6. Installed Base Momentum

Initial deployment revenue is expanding rapidly, driving up the active installed base.

As manufacturing scales, average initial ASPs per rack-equivalent are projected to compress from approximately $42,000 in 2025 down to $34,000 by 2035, improving the TCO equation but requiring vendors to extract margin from lifecycle services.

Hardware commoditization is inevitable. Value will consolidate around the system integrators who can guarantee uptime and the fluid suppliers who control the complex boiling chemistry.

Direct-to-chip is the strongest near-term substitute, absorbing mainstream AI liquid-cooling demand due to OEM alignment. Two-phase wins strictly on extreme performance. Single-phase occupies the strategic middle ground: balancing high density with practical maintainability.

8. Adoption Barrier Scorecard

Quantification of commercial headwinds restricting two-phase market penetration.

Fluid Cost & Chemistry
High Severity

High initial CAPEX for specialized boiling fluids and ongoing OPEX for vapor recovery and top-ups.

Serviceability & Operations
High Severity

Requires facility operators to manage sealed, pressurized systems, drastically altering IT maintenance workflows.

Environmental Scrutiny
High Severity

Regulatory pressure on PFAS and fluorinated fluids poses a structural risk to long-term supply chains.

OEM Compatibility
Medium Severity

Gradual improvement, but server OEMs prioritize cold-plate designs for mass-market AI architectures.

Adoption will depend entirely on resolving these friction points. GRC’s go-to-market strategy must aggressively target the "Serviceability" and "Fluid Cost" vectors, providing commercial clarity where two-phase currently offers complexity.
